Privacy Policy

How We Handle Your Information

Last updated: January 2025

Overview

Milton Advocates ("we", "us", "our") is committed to protecting your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, who we share it with, and the rights you hold in relation to it. It applies to information collected through this website and through our client engagement process.

We collect only the minimum personal information necessary. If you believe we hold information about you that is excessive or inaccurate, please contact us immediately.

Information We Collect

We may collect personal information when you:

  • Submit an enquiry or contact form on this website
  • Send us your curriculum vitae or career application
  • Instruct us in connection with a legal matter
  • Communicate with us by email, telephone, or post
  • Visit and browse this website (technical data such as IP address and browser type, collected via standard server logs)

The personal information we collect may include your name, email address, telephone number, company name, and the content of any correspondence you send us. We do not collect special category data (such as health, religious, or biometric information) through this website, and we ask that you do not submit such information to us.

How We Use Your Information

We use the personal information we collect to:

  • Respond to your enquiries and provide legal services
  • Verify your identity and manage our client relationship
  • Comply with our professional, regulatory, and legal obligations as advocates regulated under the laws of Uganda
  • Maintain and improve the operation of this website
  • Consider your application if you have submitted a CV or career enquiry

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to third parties for marketing purposes.

Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal information on one or more of the following grounds: to perform our contractual obligations to you; to comply with legal and regulatory requirements applicable to a law firm practising in Uganda; to pursue our legitimate interests in delivering legal services; or, where required, with your express consent.

Sharing of Information

We may share your personal information with:

  • Professional advisers, co-counsel, or experts engaged on your matter with your authority
  • Courts, tribunals, arbitral institutions, or regulatory bodies as required by law or by the conduct of proceedings
  • Our technology and hosting service providers, who are bound by confidentiality and data security obligations

All third parties with whom we share data are required to maintain appropriate standards of confidentiality and security consistent with those we apply ourselves.

Retention

We retain personal information for as long as is necessary for the purpose for which it was collected, or as required by applicable law or professional regulation. Client matter files are typically retained for a minimum of seven years following the conclusion of a matter, in accordance with standard legal professional practice in Uganda.

Your Rights

Subject to applicable law, you have the right to request access to, correction of, or deletion of personal information we hold about you. You may also object to certain processing or withdraw consent where processing is based on consent alone. To exercise any of these rights, contact us using the details below.

Security

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect your personal information against unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. However, no method of transmission over the internet is entirely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security of data transmitted to us electronically.

Third-Party Links

This website may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those sites and encourage you to review their privacy notices before providing any personal information.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The current version will always be available on this page, with the date of last revision noted above. Continued use of this website after any update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
